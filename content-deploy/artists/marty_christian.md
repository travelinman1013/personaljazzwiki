## Biography
Marty Christian, a native of Cleveland, Ohio, first emerged in the music scene during the early 1990s as a Blues radio DJ in his hometown. Seeking broader horizons, he relocated to Austin, Texas, where he immersed himself in the vibrant Blues community, honing his skills as a musician and bandleader. His career further evolved through stints in Lafayette, Louisiana's Zydeco scene, blending diverse regional sounds into his distinctive style before settling in New Orleans.

Christian's musical approach, often termed 'Bluesicana,' fuses deep Blues roots with Americana elements, drawing from his experiences across these musical hubs. As a solo artist and veteran of notable bands led by Blues legends Henry Gray and Carol Fran, he has cultivated a versatile sound spanning multiple genres, including acoustic Blues and original compositions. His work reflects a journey through America's rich musical landscapes, from Texas Blues to Louisiana Zydeco.

Today, based in New Orleans, Marty Christian continues to perform and record, maintaining a reputation for authentic, genre-spanning music that honors his influences while pushing creative boundaries. His legacy lies in his ability to synthesize regional styles into a cohesive, roots-driven artistry.
