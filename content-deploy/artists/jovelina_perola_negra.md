## Biography
Jovelina Pérola Negra, born Jovelina Farias Belfort on July 21, 1944, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, was a renowned samba singer and songwriter. She grew up in the Belford Roxo neighborhood and spent her early years singing and dancing samba. Until her forties, Pérola Negra worked as a housemaid while participating in community samba parties (pagodes) and as a member of the Império Serrano samba school. Her career took off in the 1980s with the new pagode movement, where she was discovered by a local producer at a pagode. She recorded her first album, 'Raça Brasileira,' in 1985 alongside other emerging artists like Zeca Pagodinho. Pérola Negra's deep, swinging voice and improvisational skills made her a standout in the partido alto samba style, earning her the nickname 'mãe do pagode' (mother of pagode). She released several successful albums, earning a platinum certificate, and performed internationally before her untimely death on November 2, 1998, at age 54. Her legacy continues to influence contemporary samba artists. Pérola Negra's life story is a testament to perseverance and talent, as she rose from humble beginnings to become one of Brazil's samba icons. Her music remains celebrated for its powerful expression and connection to the roots of Brazilian culture.
