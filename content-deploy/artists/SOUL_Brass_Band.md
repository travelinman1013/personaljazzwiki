## Biography
Soul Brass Band is a contemporary New Orleans brass ensemble founded and led by percussionist Derrick 'Smoker' Freeman. The group emerged from the vibrant musical landscape of New Orleans, blending the city's rich brass band traditions with modern influences like retro soul, Cajun, and zydeco. Freeman, a noted raconteur and performer, assembled the band alongside Billboard-charting saxophonist and vocalist Reggie Houston, among other talented local musicians, with the aim of creating a party-ready brass band that honors tradition while pushing boundaries.[4][5]

Soul Brass Band quickly became known for their energetic performances, combining classic second-line rhythms with funk, soul, and pop sensibilities. Their repertoire spans traditional brass standards and inventive covers, reflecting the multicultural tapestry of New Orleans music. The band has played at iconic venues and festivals throughout the city, often performing on balconies overlooking the French Quarter and bringing their infectious sound to both locals and tourists alike.[6][8]

Through their innovative approach, Soul Brass Band has helped revitalize the brass band scene, attracting a new generation of listeners while preserving the legacy of New Orleans' musical heritage. Their commitment to both authenticity and experimentation has established them as a staple of the city's live music circuit, celebrated for their ability to unite audiences in joyful celebration.
