## Biography
Waylon Thibodeaux, known as 'Louisiana's Rockin Fiddler,' is a celebrated Cajun musician born and raised in Houma, Louisiana, in the heart of Louisiana's Bayou Country. He began playing music at the age of eight and quickly developed a reputation for his energetic performances and mastery of Cajun fiddle. By the age of 16, he had won the title of Louisiana's state fiddle champion, marking the beginning of a career that would see him perform at festivals, clubs, and special events across the United States, Canada, France, and Central and South America. Thibodeaux is recognized for his ability to blend traditional Cajun and Zydeco sounds with a high-energy, crowd-pleasing style that has made him one of Louisiana's best-known recording artists.

Thibodeaux's musical journey is deeply rooted in the cultural traditions of his native region. His performances are characterized by their authenticity and passion, reflecting his upbringing in a community where music and dance are integral to daily life. Over the years, he has become a prominent ambassador of Cajun music, helping to preserve and promote the genre both nationally and internationally. His legacy is further cemented by his recognition as a Tradition Bearer at the annual Rougarou Festival, highlighting his role in sustaining Louisiana's rich musical heritage.
