## Biography
Jason Mingledorff is a Grammy-winning saxophonist, composer, and educator based in New Orleans, where he has been one of the city's most versatile and in-demand musicians for nearly 30 years. After earning a Bachelor of Music in classical saxophone from the University of Alabama, he moved to New Orleans in 1995 to study under jazz patriarch Ellis Marsalis, transitioning from classical training to the vibrant jazz scene.[1][3][7] His early musical experiences shaped his adaptability across genres, leading to a prolific career with appearances on over 100 recordings and extensive touring.[5][6]

Since 2006, Mingledorff has taught saxophone, improvisation, and jazz ensembles at Loyola University New Orleans, earning respect as a dedicated educator while maintaining a demanding performance schedule.[2][3][6][8] A D'Addario Woodwinds Artist since 2017, he has built a vast resume through diverse collaborations in New Orleans and beyond, solidifying his reputation as one of the hardest-working musicians in the business.[2][4][6] His Grammy win underscores his contributions to the local and national music scenes.

Mingledorff's musical style blends classical precision with jazz improvisation, reflecting his New Orleans immersion and broad stylistic range, from jazz to other genres, leaving a legacy as a versatile performer, composer, and mentor in the Crescent City's jazz community.[1][2][5]
