## Biography
Kyle Roussel is a multi-instrumentalist, composer, producer, and performer whose artistry embodies the spirit and diversity of New Orleans music. Born in Boutte, Louisiana, Roussel began playing piano at age nine, initially studying classical music before immersing himself in church performances and later exploring jazz during his teenage years. He is a graduate of the New Orleans Center for Creative Arts and the University of New Orleans, institutions that further shaped his musical foundation and exposed him to a wide array of genres and mentors.

Roussel's career has flourished through his versatility across jazz, classical, funk, gospel, rhythm and blues, and rock. He is recognized for his work on piano, organ, keyboard, and drums, both as a solo artist and as a sought-after collaborator. Roussel has toured globally, performing at renowned venues such as Carnegie Hall, Montreux Jazz Festival, and Madison Square Garden. He is a regular member of the Preservation Hall Jazz Band and The Headhunters, and has shared the stage or studio with luminaries including Jon Batiste, Dave Grohl, Paul McCartney, Terrence Blanchard, and Chance the Rapper. His 2023 album 'NOLA A La Mode' showcases his solo piano prowess, blending original compositions and covers that highlight his deep connection to New Orleans' musical heritage.

Roussel's impact extends beyond performance; he is an accomplished producer, having led projects for bands like The Headhunters and contributed to film and television, notably portraying Jelly Roll Morton in HBO's 'Interview with the Vampire.' As an ambassador for New Orleans music, Roussel continues to innovate, with forthcoming projects including a jazz-funk organ record and a vocal debut, ensuring his influence on the city's evolving soundscape.
