## Biography
Debbie Davis is a New Orleans-based singer, ukulele master, and entertainer recognized as a local institution in the music scene, often performing alongside her husband, Matt Perrine, a multi-instrumentalist with a career spanning blues, zydeco, and R&B. Together, they have collaborated on projects blending blues rock and modern blues styles, including their critically acclaimed 2014 holiday album 'Oh Crap, It's Christmas!', which originated as a Christmas gift recording session.[1][3] Davis has performed solo and with Perrine, captivating audiences with her versatile talents in the vibrant New Orleans music community.[2][6]

Matt Perrine brings extensive experience to their partnership, having spent a significant portion of his career as a guitarist with Clifton Chenier's band, making his stage debut at a young age, and later showcasing skills on sousaphone and other instruments in various regional acts.[4] The couple's joint performances and recordings highlight their shared commitment to New Orleans' rich musical traditions, including blues and holiday-themed works that resonate with local and broader audiences.[5] Their musical style reflects the city's eclectic influences, from blues to zydeco.

## Fun Facts
- Debbie Davis and Matt Perrine recorded their hit holiday album 'Oh Crap, It's Christmas!' in 2014 simply because it was the Christmas gift they could afford at the time.[3]
- Matt Perrine made his stage debut as a young guitarist with zydeco legend Clifton Chenier's band.[4]
