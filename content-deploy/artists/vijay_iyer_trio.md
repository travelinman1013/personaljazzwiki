## Biography
The Vijay Iyer Trio is a celebrated jazz ensemble led by pianist, composer, and bandleader Vijay Iyer. Formed in the early 2000s, the trio quickly gained recognition for its innovative approach to jazz, blending intricate rhythmic structures, improvisational depth, and a broad palette of influences ranging from South Indian classical music to contemporary African American piano traditions. The group’s core lineup—Vijay Iyer on piano, Marcus Gilmore on drums, and Stephan Crump on bass—has remained remarkably stable, allowing for a deep musical rapport and a signature sound that is both cerebral and emotionally resonant. Their albums, including *Historicity* (2009), *Accelerando* (2012), and *Break Stuff* (2015), have been widely acclaimed for their originality and technical mastery, earning top honors in critics’ polls and establishing the trio as a leading force in modern jazz.

Musically, the Vijay Iyer Trio is known for its dynamic interplay, genre-blurring compositions, and reinterpretations of works by artists as diverse as Stevie Wonder, Leonard Bernstein, and Michael Jackson. The trio’s style is marked by a keen sense of rhythm, a willingness to experiment with form, and a commitment to pushing the boundaries of jazz. Their performances and recordings have been praised for their energy, intelligence, and emotional depth, and the group has played at major venues and festivals around the world. The Vijay Iyer Trio’s legacy is one of innovation and influence, inspiring a new generation of jazz musicians and helping to redefine the possibilities of the piano trio format.

## Fun Facts
- The Vijay Iyer Trio was named 2015 Jazz Group of the Year in the DownBeat International Critics Poll.
- Their album Accelerando was voted #1 Jazz Album of the Year in 2012 by DownBeat, JazzTimes, and Rhapsody, and was also chosen as jazz album of the year by NPR, the Los Angeles Times, PopMatters, and Amazon.com.
- Vijay Iyer is entirely self-taught as a pianist and composer, and he earned a master’s in physics before pursuing music professionally.
- The trio’s music often incorporates elements from South Indian classical music, West African drumming, and contemporary European composers.
