## Biography
Mandy Rowden, hailing from East Texas, began her musical journey at the age of six, learning classical piano and guitar, which laid the foundation for her career as a singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist[3][5][6]. Now based in Austin, Texas, she has developed into a versatile performer known for her rock and roll influences, while also establishing herself as an educator and community builder in the music scene[1][2][4].

Rowden's career highlights include founding Girl Guitar, a women's guitar school and rock camp in Austin, which offers comprehensive programs to empower female musicians through teaching and performance opportunities[1][2][4]. Her style blends classical training with singer-songwriter sensibilities and rock energy, reflected in her work with labels like Howlin' Dog Records[6]. She continues to perform and teach, fostering a new generation of musicians.

While specific details on her full discography or major releases are limited in available sources, Rowden's legacy centers on her dual role as artist and educator, promoting accessibility in rock music for women in the Austin area[1][4].
