## Biography
Glen David Andrews is a renowned musician from New Orleans, born in the historic Tremé neighborhood, which is often considered the oldest Black community in the United States. He comes from a storied family of musicians, including his brother Derrick Tabb of the Rebirth Brass Band and his cousin Troy 'Trombone Shorty' Andrews. Andrews began his musical journey as a child, starting with the bass drum before moving to the trombone at the age of 12. He honed his skills with various brass bands such as New Birth, Li’l Rascals, and ReBirth, learning from iconic figures like Tuba Fats. His music is a powerful blend of New Orleans jazz, funk, gospel, and rock, reflecting his dedication to cultural preservation. Andrews has gained recognition for his commanding voice and fierce trombone sound, making him a beloved figure in New Orleans music. He has headlined at the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival and performed at numerous other prestigious venues. Andrews' albums, such as 'Redemption' and 'Portrait of the Treme Prince,' showcase his versatility and artistic depth. His legacy is marked by his unwavering commitment to preserving New Orleans' indigenous traditions while innovating within the city's rich musical heritage.

## Fun Facts
- Glen David Andrews was born the day after Tuba Fats directed his horn toward Andrews' mother's belly to induce labor, according to family folklore.
- Andrews began touring at the age of 14, performing in cities worldwide.
- He has made notable appearances at venues like Lincoln Center, Tipitina’s, and the Kennedy Center.
- Andrews' music is described as having the soul fire of Wilson Pickett, the preacher power of Solomon Burke, and the world-weariness of Louis Armstrong.
