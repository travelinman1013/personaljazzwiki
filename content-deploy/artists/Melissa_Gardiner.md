## Biography
Melissa Gardiner is an acclaimed jazz trombonist recognized for her technically creative and emotionally powerful performances. Raised with a classical foundation, Gardiner later embraced jazz, blending her rigorous training with a spirited improvisational style. Her artistry has been praised by jazz legend Curtis Fuller, who described her as both technically creative and emotionally powerful, highlighting her ability to inspire through improvisation.

Gardiner's career has seen her emerge as a prominent figure in the jazz and funk scenes, particularly noted for her album 'Empowered,' which showcases a dynamic mix of jazz, funk, and atmospheric tracks. She is also an educator, serving on the faculty of Opus Ithaca School of Music, where she mentors aspiring musicians. Gardiner's approach to jazz is marked by a fusion of classical discipline and expressive jazz phrasing, making her a standout among contemporary trombonists.

Her influence extends beyond performance, as she is committed to empowering the next generation of musicians through education and collaboration. Gardiner's legacy is being built not only through her recordings and live performances but also through her dedication to teaching and inspiring others in the jazz community.
