## Biography
Herlin Riley, born on February 15, 1957, in New Orleans, Louisiana, is a renowned American jazz drummer, percussionist, and composer. He was raised in a musical family and began playing drums at the age of three. Riley initially studied trumpet through high school and college but eventually focused on drums. His early career included performances with local bands in New Orleans before joining Ahmad Jamal's group in the early 1980s. This collaboration garnered critical acclaim and showcased his unique blend of precision and creativity. In 1988, Riley joined Wynton Marsalis and became a cornerstone of the Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra, solidifying his reputation as a leading interpreter of traditional and modern jazz. His playing style is characterized by an infectious swing and dynamic phrasing, seamlessly blending African, Latin, and second-line rhythms into jazz. Beyond performance, Riley is a dedicated educator, having taught masterclasses worldwide and sharing his knowledge of jazz drumming and New Orleans traditions.
