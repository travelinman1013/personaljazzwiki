## Biography
The Ginger Baker Trio was formed in 1994 as a jazz project featuring legendary drummer Peter Edward 'Ginger' Baker alongside bassist Charlie Haden and guitarist Bill Frisell.[3] Baker, born on 19 August 1939 in London, had initially gained worldwide fame as the drummer of Cream in the 1960s, where he revolutionized rock drumming by melding jazz and African rhythms into a pioneering fusion sound.[3] However, Baker's true passion had always been jazz, and the Ginger Baker Trio represented his deliberate return to his musical roots after decades of rock stardom. The trio's work showcased Baker's sophisticated understanding of complex jazz patterns, polyrhythms, and world music influences, creating a unique blend of jazz, folk, and Middle Eastern musical elements that demonstrated his evolution as a musician beyond his rock icon status.[2]

The Ginger Baker Trio released the album 'Going Back Home,' which featured intricate rhythmic interplay between Baker's virtuosic double bass drum kit work and tom-tom fills, Haden's warm and melodic bass lines, and Frisell's ethereal, impressionistic guitar textures.[2] The album's tracklist included compositions such as 'Rambler,' 'I Lu Kron,' 'Straight No Chaser,' 'Ramblin',' and 'Ginger Blues,' each showcasing the trio's ability to blend improvisation with composition across multiple genres.[2] This project solidified Baker's legacy not merely as a rock superstar but as a serious jazz musician whose influence extended far beyond the confines of commercial rock music. The Ginger Baker Trio became essential listening for jazz enthusiasts and drumming aficionados, proving that Baker's artistic journey was one of continuous evolution and boundary-pushing throughout his career.[2]

Baker's later years saw continued jazz exploration, including his 2013-2014 tours with the Ginger Baker Jazz Confusion, a quartet featuring saxophonist Alfred 'Pee Wee' Ellis, bassist Alec Dankworth, and percussionist Abass Dodoo.[3] His final album, 'Why?,' was released in 2014 under the Motéma Music label, representing the culmination of his storied career and an amalgamation of his jazz and African music influences.[1] Baker passed away on 6 October 2019, leaving behind a multifaceted legacy that transcended genre boundaries and demonstrated the profound impact of jazz-influenced drumming on modern music.

## Fun Facts
- Baker once attacked bassist Jack Bruce with a knife during a concert, reflecting the extreme volatility of their relationship despite their musical partnership in multiple bands.[3]
- Baker lived in Parker, Colorado between 1993 and 1999, where he was passionate about polo and sponsored an ongoing series of jam sessions and concerts at the Salisbury Equestrian Park on weekends.[3]
- In its two-year existence, Cream sold over 15 million records and played to adoring crowds, breaking box office records previously held by The Beatles, largely due to Baker's innovative drumming contributions.[4]
- Baker's autobiography, titled 'Hellraiser,' was published in 2009, documenting his tumultuous career marked by drug dependency and his journey from aspiring jazzman to rock demigod.[3]
