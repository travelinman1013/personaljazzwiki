## Biography
Bailey Flores is a New Orleans-based indie pop singer-songwriter originally from Luling, Louisiana, a small town near New Orleans. She began her musical journey as a junior in high school, starting to write music and develop her distinctive sound characterized by captivating melodies, thoughtful lyrics, and dreamy, ethereal qualities. A self-confessed emo kid, Flores draws from a stylistically varied palette, blending indie pop with emotional depth, exploring themes of love, heartbreak, self-discovery, and the human experience with raw vulnerability.[1][4][6]

Flores has built a growing following through her versatile releases, including standout tracks like 'Grave Dancer,' 'Savory,' 'Kiss Me More,' and 'Our Time,' as well as remixes such as 'All Go Wrong (MZG Remix)' and 'Running Out (WODD Remix).' She serves as the founder and director of Bailey Flores Entertainment, LLC, and works professionally as a songwriter, topliner, and voice actor, collaborating across genres like pop and electronic music. Her voice adapts fluidly, from sultry covers like Doja Cat to upbeat dance tracks, showcasing her uncategorizable style.[1][2][3][5]

While still emerging with Spotify popularity around 20, Flores is establishing herself as a force in indie pop, with recent activity including remixes of tracks by Defunk, WODD, and MZG, hinting at future growth in the industry.
