## Biography
Plunge is a Swedish jazz trio formed in Malmö in 2001, known for their collective improvisation and acoustic music rooted in the Scandinavian jazz tradition. The group consists of saxophonist Andreas Andersson, double bassist Mattias Hjorth, and drummer Peter Nilsson. Their music is characterized by patient, spontaneous improvisation, often blending structured compositions with free-form exploration, resulting in a sound that is both introspective and dynamic. Over the years, Plunge has developed a reputation for their live performances and recordings, which highlight their deep musical rapport and commitment to innovation within the jazz idiom.

The trio's career has been marked by a series of critically acclaimed albums and collaborations, most notably with the renowned Swedish pianist Bobo Stenson. Their partnership with Stenson began in 2002 and has produced several recordings and tours, enriching both the trio's sound and their standing in the European jazz scene. Plunge has toured extensively throughout Sweden and Europe, appearing at major festivals and venues, and their work has been featured on national and international radio broadcasts. Their legacy lies in their contribution to the evolution of contemporary Scandinavian jazz, particularly through their emphasis on collective improvisation and their openness to collaboration.
