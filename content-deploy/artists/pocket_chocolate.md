## Biography
Pocket Chocolate is a seven-piece funk, blues, and soul collective originating from New Orleans, Louisiana. Formed in 2019, the band consists of Tucker France (guitar, backing vocals), Gabe France (bass, lead vocals), Connor Zaeringer (drums), and Garret Hickman (saxophone), among other local musicians. Drawing from the rich musical heritage of their hometown, Pocket Chocolate quickly established themselves on the Louisiana live music circuit, performing at venues and festivals throughout the region.

The band's sound is deeply rooted in the traditions of blues rock, modern blues, Cajun, and zydeco, blending these genres with a modern funk and soul sensibility. Their energetic performances and tight musicianship reflect both reverence for classic New Orleans styles and a willingness to experiment with contemporary grooves. Pocket Chocolate released their first studio LP in 2024, featuring tracks like 'Tell Me Twice,' 'Nu Funky Thang,' and 'Waxing Gibbous,' which showcase their dynamic range and commitment to authentic, danceable music.

Though still early in their career, Pocket Chocolate has become known for their vibrant live shows and collaborative spirit, embodying the communal ethos of New Orleans music. Their legacy is being built through grassroots engagement with fans and a dedication to keeping the city's funk, blues, and soul traditions alive for new generations.
