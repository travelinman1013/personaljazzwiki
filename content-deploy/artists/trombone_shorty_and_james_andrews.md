## Biography
Troy 'Trombone Shorty' Andrews and his brother James Andrews are both renowned musicians from New Orleans, steeped in the city's rich musical heritage. Born on January 2, 1986, Trombone Shorty began performing at a young age, earning his nickname due to his small stature compared to his trombone. He grew up in the Tremé neighborhood, surrounded by influential musicians, including his grandfather Jesse Hill, known for 'Ooh Poo Pah Doo.' James Andrews, born January 12, 1969, is a trumpeter and vocalist, often referred to as the 'Satchmo of the Ghetto.' He played a significant role in raising and mentoring Trombone Shorty, exposing him to various musical opportunities. Both brothers have contributed significantly to New Orleans' jazz and brass band traditions, with Trombone Shorty achieving international fame for his eclectic style, blending jazz, funk, rock, and hip-hop. James Andrews has also had a notable career, releasing albums like 'Satchmo of the Ghetto' and performing with his band, the Crescent City Allstars. After Hurricane Katrina, both brothers were instrumental in promoting New Orleans' cultural recovery through music. Trombone Shorty's band, Orleans Avenue, has become a staple of New Orleans music, while James Andrews continues to perform and inspire new generations of musicians.

## Fun Facts
- Trombone Shorty got his nickname because he was shorter than his trombone when he started playing at age four.
- James Andrews was one of the first musicians to return to New Orleans after Hurricane Katrina, performing at Jackson Square.
- Trombone Shorty's music is a blend of jazz, funk, rock, and hip-hop, reflecting New Orleans' diverse musical heritage.
- The Andrews family opened a nightclub in Tremé called Trombone Shorty's, where Troy would occasionally perform as a child.
