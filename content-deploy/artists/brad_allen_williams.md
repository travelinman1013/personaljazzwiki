## Biography
Brad Allen Williams is an American guitarist, composer, producer, and songwriter born on September 10, 1980, in Memphis, Tennessee. Raised in the rich musical environment of Memphis, Williams developed a deep appreciation for a wide range of genres, which would later inform his eclectic and nuanced approach to music. He describes himself as a 'professional listener,' a philosophy that has shaped his multifaceted career as a recording artist, session musician, and creative collaborator.[4][5][1]

Williams has become known for his surrealistic guitar stylism and his ability to serve music in many capacities, including as a producer and mixer. He has worked with a diverse array of artists across genres, including Cory Henry, Bilal, Kris Bowers, and José James, contributing both as a guitarist and as a creative force behind the scenes. Splitting his time between Los Angeles and New York City, Williams is recognized for his technical proficiency, emotional expressiveness, and innovative soundscapes, making him a sought-after collaborator in contemporary jazz, soul, and beyond.[3][6][7]

His musical style is characterized by a blend of jazz, soul, R&B, and experimental elements, reflecting his Memphis roots and his openness to new sonic possibilities. Williams' legacy is defined by his versatility and his commitment to elevating the work of others, whether as a bandleader, sideman, or producer, earning him a reputation as a musician's musician in the modern music landscape.[2][7]
