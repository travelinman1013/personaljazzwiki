## Biography
Left Lane Cruiser is an American blues rock band originating from Fort Wayne, Indiana.  The band's core consists of Fredrick "Joe" Evans IV on slide guitar and vocals, and Brenn Beck on drums and backing vocals.  Their musical foundation is deeply rooted in the North Mississippi Hill country blues tradition, drawing significant inspiration from artists associated with Fat Possum Records.  The band's formation and early years are not detailed in the provided source.

Left Lane Cruiser's career took a significant leap forward in July 2007 with their signing to Alive Records.  Their debut nationally released album, *Bring Yo' Ass to the Table*, followed in January 2008.  This was quickly followed by their first European tour in September 2008, which faced initial setbacks due to passport issues.  Subsequent releases included *All You Can Eat* in September 2009, featuring the song "Waynedale" which gained exposure through its use in the television series *Breaking Bad*.  Their fourth album, released March 1, 2011, included collaborations with John Wesley Myers, keyboard player for the Black Diamond Heavies, another Alive Records artist.

Left Lane Cruiser's musical style is characterized by its strong foundation in North Mississippi Hill country blues.  The band's specific innovations or lasting legacy are not detailed in the provided source.  However, their appearance on *Breaking Bad* demonstrates a level of mainstream recognition.
