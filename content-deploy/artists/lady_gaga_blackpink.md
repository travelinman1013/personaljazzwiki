## Biography
Lady Gaga and Blackpink are two globally renowned musical acts who have each made significant impacts in the pop and K-pop music scenes. Lady Gaga, born Stefani Joanne Angelina Germanotta in New York City, rose to fame in the late 2000s with her bold persona and genre-blending pop music, quickly becoming a cultural icon known for her theatrical performances and advocacy for LGBTQ+ rights. Blackpink, formed in 2016 by YG Entertainment, is a South Korean girl group consisting of members Jisoo, Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa. They have become one of the most successful K-pop groups internationally, known for their powerful vocals, dynamic choreography, and fusion of pop, hip-hop, and EDM elements. Their collaboration on the song 'Sour Candy' in 2020 marked a historic moment in music, bridging Western pop and K-pop audiences and showcasing the global reach of both artists.

Musically, Lady Gaga is celebrated for her versatility, spanning pop, dance, electronic, and even jazz, while Blackpink is recognized for their signature blend of catchy hooks, rap verses, and visually striking music videos. Both have pushed boundaries in their respective genres, with Lady Gaga earning accolades for her songwriting and acting, and Blackpink breaking records as the first K-pop girl group to headline major Western festivals. Their legacy is defined by innovation, empowerment, and a commitment to artistic expression, inspiring millions worldwide.

## Fun Facts
- Lady Gaga and Blackpink's 'Sour Candy' became Blackpink's first top-ten single in Australia and their first top-twenty single in the UK.
- The song 'Sour Candy' was inspired by the concept of Sour Patch Kids, with the lyrics playing on the idea of being sour then sweet.
- Lady Gaga performed 'Sour Candy' live for the first time during her Chromatica Ball stadium tour in 2022.
- Blackpink's members became fans of Lady Gaga before collaborating, and the group's management described the project as a natural collaboration between mutual admirers.
