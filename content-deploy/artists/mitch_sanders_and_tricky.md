## Biography
Mitch Sanders is a Bristol-born singer-songwriter who has rapidly emerged as a compelling new voice in British indie music. Before his music career took off, Sanders worked on construction sites, saving money from his bricklaying job to fund studio sessions in London. Deeply influenced by his upbringing and community in Bristol, his early work, including the debut single 'Garden of England,' reflects themes of family, belonging, and the rugged landscapes of his heritage in County Mayo. Sanders’ music is known for its cinematic, emotionally raw sound, blending acoustic and electronic elements with rich storytelling.

Sanders’ career development accelerated after signing with Island Records and collaborating with notable producers and musicians. His EPs, including 'These Things We Carry' and 'Saloon,' showcase a progression from introspective explorations of family and place to more personal, soul-searching themes. Sanders credits his father as a significant influence and support throughout his journey. His vocal style, shaped by years of listening to artists like Sam Cooke, Otis Redding, Joni Mitchell, and David Bowie, is marked by emotive delivery and a blend of soul and indie sensibilities. Critics have highlighted his voice and honest lyricism as defining features of his artistry.

Despite his growing recognition, Sanders remains grounded, valuing authentic connection over commercial success. He continues to draw inspiration from everyday life, aiming to create music that resonates universally while staying true to his roots in Bristol.
