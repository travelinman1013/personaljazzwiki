## Biography
Big Daddy 'O', born Owen Tufts, is a prominent blues musician hailing from the delta region near the Louisiana/Mississippi border. Standing at 6’6”, his commanding stage presence is matched by a powerful and expressive voice that ranges from a robust shout to a gentle whisper. Tufts began his musical journey at age 19 as a street musician in the French Quarter of New Orleans, absorbing influences and techniques from the vibrant local scene. He briefly attended LSUNO before dedicating himself fully to music, performing in roadhouses and bars for over three decades.

His career took a significant turn when Rabadash Records discovered him at Betty’s Bar in Amite, Louisiana around the year 2000. Since then, Big Daddy 'O' has released several critically acclaimed albums, including "Used Blues" in 2010, which showcased his hard-driving blues style and electric guitar prowess. He is known for making every song his own, whether performing solo with his custom acoustic guitar or leading a full blues band with his vintage 1969 Les Paul Deluxe. His music has reached national audiences and has even been featured in the award-winning animated film "The Magistical."

Big Daddy 'O' is celebrated for his authentic approach to blues, blending traditional influences with his own heartfelt delivery. His legacy is rooted in the Louisiana blues tradition, and his performances are characterized by warmth, sincerity, and a deep connection with his audience. Supported by his wife Gretchen, who manages merchandise and fan interactions at gigs, Big Daddy 'O' has built a loyal following and continues to be a beloved figure in the regional and national blues community.
