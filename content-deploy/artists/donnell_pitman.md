## Biography
Donnell Pitman, a soul/disco vocalist, was born in St. Louis, Missouri, and raised in rural Cairo, Illinois, at the state's extreme southern tip. He arrived in Chicago in 1967, where he formed his first group and began his music career during the vibrant disco era.[3] Known primarily for his infectious rarity 'Love Explosion,' released in 1980 on the Arpco label, Pitman became a Chicago star whose work has been championed in modern disco circles.[1][2][6]

Pitman's musical style is rooted in soul and post-disco, with his 7-inch single 'Love Explosion' emerging as one of the most coveted records across genres due to its rarity and appeal.[2][6] His career saw a revival through reissues on specialty labels, leading to performances like his return to Chicago's The Hideout for a disco-era revival event.[1] As a vocalist from the Chicago scene, his contributions highlight the city's rich party and dance music history.[3]

Though details on his full discography and later career are limited, Pitman's legacy endures among collectors and enthusiasts, with recent reissues keeping his ultra-high-frequency sound alive in contemporary sets.[1][6]
