## Biography
Carlo Ditta is a **Grammy-nominated New Orleans-based producer, songwriter, and guitarist** who has been a vital figure in the Crescent City music scene for over 30 years.[3][4] As the founder and owner of Orleans Records, established in the 1990s, Ditta has specialized in producing blues, R&B, jazz, soul, and funk music, working with numerous regional and national artists.[3][5] Beyond his production work, Ditta is recognized as a remarkable singer and songwriter in his own right, contributing both instrumental and vocal performances to various projects.[1] His career reflects a deep commitment to preserving and advancing New Orleans' rich musical heritage, positioning him as one of the city's most respected independent record producers despite remaining relatively unknown outside music industry circles.[6]

## Fun Facts
- Carlo Ditta is credited as an archivist who helps preserve New Orleans music history, reflecting his broader commitment to the cultural legacy of the city beyond his production work.[9]
- In addition to his production and songwriting roles, Ditta has performed multiple instruments on recordings, including guitar and background vocals, demonstrating his versatility as a musician.[5]
- Orleans Records, Ditta's label founded in the 1990s, was recognized as being "in the very first rank of New Orleans record companies active in the '90s," establishing his prominence during a pivotal era for independent music production.[5]
- Ditta has lived in multiple cities including New York City and Nashville for periods of time, though he is primarily based in New Orleans where he has built his career and reputation.[8]
