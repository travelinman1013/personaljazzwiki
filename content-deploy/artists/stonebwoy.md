## Biography
Livingstone Etse Satekla, known professionally as Stonebwoy, was born on March 5, 1988, in Ashaiman, a suburb of Accra, Ghana. From an early age, he demonstrated a passion for the creative arts, writing songs and acting in school drama clubs by the fourth grade. Stonebwoy’s musical journey began in earnest during his high school years, where he gained underground popularity through rap battles and released his first radio hit, 'Makuma (My Heart)'.

Stonebwoy’s career took off after joining Samini’s HighGrade Family in 2008, where he honed his craft before founding his own label, Burniton Music Group, in 2012. His debut album, 'Grade 1', was followed by 'Necessary Evil', 'Epistles of Mama', and 'Anloga Junction', each solidifying his reputation as a leading figure in Afropop, dancehall, and reggae. In 2022, he signed with Universal Music Group’s Def Jam Recordings, releasing '5Th Dimension', which debuted at number eight on the Billboard Reggae Albums Chart. Stonebwoy is celebrated for his dynamic blend of Afrobeats, reggae, dancehall, and Ghanaian highlife, often performing in Jamaican Patois and collaborating with artists across Africa, the UK, and Jamaica.

Stonebwoy’s legacy is marked by numerous accolades, including the 2015 BET Award for Best International Act: Africa, multiple Ghana Music Awards, and recognition from Billboard and the Grammy Awards for his contributions to global music. Beyond music, he is a prominent advocate for sanitation and environmental issues in Ghana, and has served as a brand ambassador for major companies. Stonebwoy is widely regarded as a pioneer of Afrodancehall, elevating the genre to international prominence.
