## Biography
DJ Maphorisa, born Themba Sonnyboy Sekowe on November 15, 1987, in Soshanguve, Pretoria, South Africa, is a highly influential DJ, record producer, singer, and songwriter. Raised in a musically rich environment—his mother was a gospel singer—Maphorisa was exposed to diverse genres from an early age, which later shaped his eclectic production style. He began his career as a member of the group Uhuru, contributing to major hits like 'Y-Tjukutja' and 'Khona,' which blended traditional African sounds with contemporary beats and established his reputation in the South African music scene.[4]

Maphorisa's solo career skyrocketed as he became a leading force in the evolution of amapiano, house, and gqom music. He achieved international acclaim by co-producing Drake's global hit 'One Dance' and collaborating with artists such as Wizkid, Major Lazer, and Black Coffee. His projects, including 'Scorpion Kings' with Kabza De Small and 'Rumble in the Jungle' with TRESOR and Kabza De Small, have topped charts and won numerous awards. Maphorisa is also recognized for his entrepreneurial spirit, founding BlaqBoy Music to foster new talent and innovate within the industry. His influence extends beyond music production, as he has played a pivotal role in shaping the business landscape of South African music.[1][2][4][5]

DJ Maphorisa's legacy is defined by his genre-blending approach, mentorship of emerging artists, and global reach. He has performed at major festivals like Coachella and Glastonbury, received accolades such as the South African Music Award for Male Artist of the Year, and was the first African DJ to appear on the cover of Forbes Africa. His impact on the amapiano genre and the broader African music industry is widely acknowledged, making him a trailblazer and visionary in contemporary music.[1][2][4][5]
