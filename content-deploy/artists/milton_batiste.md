## Biography
Milton Batiste was born on September 5, 1934, in New Orleans, Louisiana, into the renowned Batiste musical family, which has produced over 25 musicians across generations. Growing up immersed in New Orleans' vibrant music culture, he became a key figure in the local jazz scene, particularly known for his work with the Olympia Brass Band, embodying the traditional sounds of the city's brass band heritage. His career spanned decades, contributing to the preservation and performance of New Orleans jazz in its classic form.

Batiste's musical style was rooted in **New Orleans Jazz** and **Band Music**, characterized by the energetic, communal brass traditions of second-line parades and street performances. As a trumpeter and bandleader, he helped maintain the Olympia Brass Band's legacy during a time when traditional jazz faced evolving influences. He performed extensively in New Orleans, bridging historical styles with live communal celebrations central to the city's identity.

Milton Batiste passed away on March 29, 2001, in New Orleans at age 66, leaving behind a lifetime of contributions to jazz. His connections within the expansive Batiste family and the broader New Orleans music community ensured his influence endured, with friends from diverse musical worlds honoring him at his passing. His work solidified his place in the lineage of musicians who defined the sound of New Orleans.
