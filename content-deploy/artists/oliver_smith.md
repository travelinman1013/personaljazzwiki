## Biography
Oliver Smith is a London-based DJ and producer renowned for his pioneering work in progressive trance and progressive house. He emerged as a significant figure in the electronic music scene in the early 2000s, notably as the first artist signed to the influential Anjunabeats label. Smith's early career was marked by his partnership in the acclaimed duo Smith & Pledger, which helped cement his reputation for melodic, emotive trance productions[2][6].

Over the past two decades, Smith has released more than 50 records and remixes, with standout solo tracks such as "Lovingly," "Warehouse," and "Be Alone" becoming staples in trance sets worldwide[1][3]. His music is regularly supported by top-tier DJs including Above & Beyond, Armin van Buuren, Paul Van Dyk, and Eric Prydz[1][2]. Smith's style is characterized by lush melodies, driving rhythms, and a polished, uplifting sound that has resonated with audiences at major festivals like Tomorrowland, EDC Las Vegas, and Creamfields, as well as iconic venues such as Ministry of Sound and Wembley Arena[1][2].

Smith's legacy is closely tied to Anjunabeats, where he remains the only producer besides label founders Above & Beyond to appear on all 16 volumes of their flagship compilation series[1][5]. His influence extends beyond his own productions, as he has helped shape the sound of modern trance and progressive house through his remixes, collaborations, and mentorship within the Anjuna community.

## Fun Facts
- Oliver Smith is the only artist besides Above & Beyond to appear on every Anjunabeats Volume compilation[1][5].
- He mixed the 'Anjunabeats Worldwide 08' compilation, which included ten of his own tracks and reached #1 on iTunes dance charts in multiple countries[1].
- Smith has performed at legendary venues such as Ministry of Sound, Hollywood Palladium, and Wembley Arena[1][2].
- He was described by Armin van Buuren as 'an excellent DJ' and by Mixmag as 'the best thing to happen in trance'[1][2].
