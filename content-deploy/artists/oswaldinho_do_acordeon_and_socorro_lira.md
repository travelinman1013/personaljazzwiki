## Biography
Oswaldinho do Acordeon & Socorro Lira is a collaborative project between two prominent Brazilian musicians, each with a rich individual legacy in the traditional forró and broader Brazilian popular music scenes. Socorro Lira, born Maria do Socorro Pereira in 1974 in the rural area of Brejo do Cruz, Paraíba, is a celebrated poet, composer, singer, instrumentalist, and cultural producer. Her career is marked by a deep engagement with the musical traditions of northeastern Brazil, blending poetic lyricism with regional rhythms. She has been recognized with several awards, including the Prêmio da Música Brasileira, and is known for her contributions to both music and literature, having published books in poetry, romance, and children's genres.[1][4][5]

Oswaldinho do Acordeon is a master accordionist renowned for his virtuosity and dedication to the forró genre, particularly the baião style popularized by Luiz Gonzaga. Together, Oswaldinho and Socorro Lira released the album 'O Samba do Rei do Baião' in 2013, a tribute to the legacy of Luiz Gonzaga, the 'King of Baião.' Their collaboration highlights a fusion of traditional forró with contemporary interpretations, showcasing both artists' commitment to preserving and evolving Brazil's musical heritage.[3][2]

Their work as a duo is characterized by a deep respect for the roots of northeastern Brazilian music, while also embracing innovation and poetic expression. Through their partnership, they have contributed to the ongoing vitality of forró, inspiring new generations of musicians and listeners.

## Fun Facts
- Socorro Lira has published five books spanning poetry, romance, and children's literature.[5]
- She co-founded and ran the Prêmio Grão de Música for ten years, promoting Brazilian songwriting alongside Elifas Andreato.[5]
- The album 'O Samba do Rei do Baião' is a direct homage to Luiz Gonzaga, a foundational figure in Brazilian music.[3]
- Oswaldinho do Acordeon is recognized as one of Brazil's foremost accordionists, celebrated for his technical mastery and contributions to the forró tradition.
