## Biography
Travis Matte is a multi-instrumentalist Cajun and zydeco musician born in 1973 in Church Point, Louisiana, who grew up immersed in Cajun French culture and music. He initially established himself as a top Cajun fiddler before expanding his musical horizons by mastering the accordion and forming Travis Matte and the Zydeco Kingpins, a band that blends traditional Cajun influences with contemporary party music. Over the past decade, Matte has built a successful career performing at dance halls and festivals throughout South Louisiana and Southeast Texas, becoming known for energetic party songs that honor the 1960s Cajun dance hall tradition while incorporating modern sensibilities.

Matte's musical style reflects the sound of 1960s Cajun dance halls, an era that deeply influenced him despite being born after that period. His work as a multi-instrumentalist demonstrates technical proficiency across multiple instruments, and his band has become a fixture in the regional South Louisiana music scene. Matte has earned significant recognition within the Cajun music community, including multiple Fiddler of the Year awards from the Cajun French Music Association, cementing his status as a respected figure in preserving and evolving Cajun musical traditions.
