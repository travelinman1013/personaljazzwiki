## Biography
Marcelo Jeneci is a Brazilian singer, songwriter, and composer born on April 7, 1982, in São Paulo, Brazil. Raised in a musically inclined family, Jeneci’s early exposure to Brazilian popular music (MPB) and traditional rhythms shaped his artistic sensibilities. He began his professional career as an accordionist, notably playing in Chico César’s band, which provided him with foundational experience in the Brazilian music scene.

Jeneci’s solo career took off with the release of his debut album 'Feito Pra Acabar' in 2010, featuring the vocalist Laura Lavieri throughout the record. His music is characterized by a unique blend of modern pop, traditional Brazilian genres, and Latin influences, marked by expressive melodies and poetic lyrics. His 2014 album 'De Graça' was nominated for a Latin Grammy for Best Brazilian Popular Music Album, cementing his reputation as a leading figure in contemporary MPB. Jeneci’s work is celebrated for its emotional depth, innovative arrangements, and ability to bridge generations of Brazilian music lovers.

Throughout his career, Jeneci has collaborated with prominent artists and continually evolved his sound, as seen in later albums like 'Guaia' (2019). His legacy is defined by his commitment to musical innovation and his role in revitalizing and expanding the reach of MPB for new audiences.

## Fun Facts
- Marcelo Jeneci’s parents are Glória da Silva and Manoel Jeneci da Silva.
- He allowed Laura Lavieri to provide vocals for his entire debut album, a rare move for a solo artist.
- His album 'De Graça' was nominated for a Latin Grammy and he won the APCA Award for Best Composer in 2014.
- Jeneci’s music often features the accordion, reflecting his early roots and adding a distinctive sound to his recordings.
