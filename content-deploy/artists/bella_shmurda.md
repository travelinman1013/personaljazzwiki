## Biography
Bella Shmurda, born Abiola Ahmed Akinbiyi on January 27, 1997, in Ikorodu, Lagos State, Nigeria, is a prominent Nigerian singer and songwriter known for his conscious afrobeats style[1][4]. Raised in a polygamous family as the youngest of ten children, he began his musical journey at Lagos State University Staff School, participating in the school band and cultural dance group[4]. He later attended Badagry Grammar School and earned a bachelor's degree in History and International Relations from Lagos State University[1][4].

Bella Shmurda's rise to fame began in 2018 with his debut single "Vision 2020," which gained widespread attention after a viral video by his friend Poco Lee and a remix featuring Nigerian rap icon Olamide[3][5]. This breakthrough led to collaborations with top Nigerian artists and the release of his first EP, "High Tension," in 2020[3][5]. Bella's music is deeply rooted in the struggles and realities of Nigerian youth, blending afrobeats, afropop, and street-conscious lyrics to create a relatable and impactful sound[3][5].

As the founder of Dangbana Republik, a creative collective in Lagos, Bella Shmurda has established himself as a leading voice for social change and youth empowerment[3][4]. His legacy is marked by over 80 songs, hundreds of millions of streams, and a reputation for addressing social issues through his music. Bella continues to influence the Nigerian music scene with his energetic performances, genre-blending style, and commitment to representing the voice of the streets[3][5].
