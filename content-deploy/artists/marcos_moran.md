## Biography
Ananias Marques de Oliveira, known as Marcos Moran, was born in 1938 in Alegre, Espírito Santo, Brazil. He lived there for about 14 years before moving to Rio de Janeiro, where he began his musical career. In 1959, he formed the group Dry Boy's with friends including Wilson Simonal, achieving relative success before the group disbanded. Moran then performed at venues like the Copacabana Palace and boate Arpèrge, leading to his signing with RCA, where he released his first compact in 1965. His early style rooted in bossa nova evolved in the 1960s, incorporating rock, soul music, samba, and marchinhas carnavalescas; his voice was often compared to his friend Wilson Simonal, who even named one of Moran's children.[1]

Moran gained prominence as an interpreter of sambas-enredo starting in 1970, invited by Carlos Imperial to sing 'Lendas e mistérios da Amazônia' for Portela. He performed for Império Serrano in 1975 with 'Zaquia Jorge, a vedete do subúrbio, a estrela de Madureira,' and from 1979 to 1984 was the voice of Unidos de Vila Isabel, delivering classics like 'Sonho de um Sonho' (1980) and 'Pra tudo se acabar na quarta-feira' (1984). He left after a dispute with a harmony director. Sporadic appearances followed, including for Acadêmicos de Santa Cruz (1989), Unidos de Lucas (1990), and his last in 2017 with Acadêmicos de Pilares. As a composer, he partnered with Roberto Carlos on 'Professor de Amor' and wrote for artists like Elza Soares ('Auera'), Cauby Peixoto ('Garota Demais'), and Altemar Dutra ('Domingo de Carnaval'). His discography includes albums like 'Do Jeito que a Gente Quer' (1971) and 'Transasamba: Marcos Moran e Samba Som Sete' (1975).[1]

Though less active later, Moran's legacy endures in samba-enredo and Brazilian popular music, blending bossa nova origins with samba traditions and international influences, cementing his role in carnival culture and recordings.[1]

## Fun Facts
- His voice in the 1960s was frequently compared to that of his close friend Wilson Simonal, who named one of Moran's children.
- He left Unidos de Vila Isabel after a dispute with a harmony director during a Sapucaí parade in 1984.
- Composed 'Auera' for Elza Soares' 1975 album 'Nos Braços do Samba' and 'Garota Demais' for Cauby Peixoto.
- Achieved carnival success with the 1968 marcha 'Até quarta-feira'.
