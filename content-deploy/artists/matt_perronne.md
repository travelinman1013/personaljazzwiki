## Biography
Matt Perronne is a trumpeter, bandleader, and composer based in New Orleans, renowned for his dynamic contributions to the city’s vibrant jazz and brass band scene. Perronne began his musical journey performing with various brass bands in New Orleans, quickly establishing himself as a versatile and expressive trumpeter. His style draws heavily on the traditions of New Orleans jazz, blending classic melodies with modern improvisational flair, and he is noted for infusing his performances with the spirit and energy of the city’s storied musical heritage.

Perronne's career reached a new level of recognition as a member of the DUKES of Dixieland, one of New Orleans’ most celebrated jazz ensembles. With the Dukes, he has performed at major venues, contributed to numerous recordings, and participated in themed concerts that celebrate the full spectrum of New Orleans music, from traditional jazz to rhythm and blues. His work as a composer and bandleader is exemplified in projects like the album "Making Space," where his composition "Gravity’s Conflict" showcases his ability to merge influences from jazz greats like John Coltrane with the exuberance of New Orleans brass bands, resulting in music that is both innovative and deeply rooted in tradition[1][3][6].

Perronne’s legacy is marked by his commitment to both preserving and expanding the New Orleans jazz tradition. Through his performances, recordings, and collaborations, he continues to inspire audiences and fellow musicians, ensuring that the city’s unique musical voice remains vital and relevant.
