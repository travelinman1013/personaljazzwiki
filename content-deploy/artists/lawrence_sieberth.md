## Biography
Lawrence Sieberth is a pianist, composer, and producer based in New Orleans whose musical career spans multiple decades and genres. Born in Detroit but raised in Baton Rouge from age five, Sieberth attended Loyola University in New Orleans, where he developed his foundational musical training. He has established himself as a versatile musician equally comfortable in jazz, classical, world music, and experimental settings, refusing to be confined by genre boundaries. His neo-bop improvisations combined with classical and world music influences have created an extensive musical vocabulary that he applies to both live performances and compositions for television, film, and stage.

Throughout his career, Sieberth has become a fixture in the New Orleans music scene, performing at virtually every venue in the city and serving as a regular featured artist and musical director at the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival for over 30 years. His collaborations with notable local performers include legendary artists such as Johnny Adams, Charles Neville, Allen Toussaint, Irma Thomas, Tony Dagradi, John Vidacovich, Herlin Riley, Victor Goines, and Don Vappie. Beyond New Orleans, Sieberth has created international projects, including forming a European quartet for his album 'An Evening in Paris,' demonstrating his ability to work across geographical and cultural boundaries.

Sieberth's musical identity reflects his philosophy of integration—combining neo-bop improvisations with experimental inclinations to create engaging, inclusive musical experiences. His work encompasses diverse ensembles ranging from New Orleans traditional music to avant-garde improvisations, mainstream jazz, and R&B. As a multifaceted musician, he has worked as a pianist, composer, arranger, musical director, educator, and producer, earning recognition as a veteran player with extensive national and international credits as a versatile keyboard accompanist.

## Fun Facts
- Despite being born in Detroit, Sieberth moved to Baton Rouge at age five and has spent most of his life in Louisiana, becoming deeply embedded in the New Orleans music culture.
- Sieberth has been a regular featured artist and musical director at the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival for over 30 years, making him one of the festival's most consistent contributors.
- He created an international European quartet specifically for his album 'An Evening in Paris,' demonstrating his ability to conceptualize and execute projects across geographical and cultural boundaries.
- Sieberth's musical philosophy explicitly rejects genre boundaries—he has stated that he 'never met a musical style he didn't like or to which he couldn't add his own personal stamp,' working across jazz, classical, world music, avant-garde, and R&B.
