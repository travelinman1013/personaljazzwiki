## Biography
Adriana Moreira is a distinguished Brazilian samba singer and composer, born on March 5, 1970, in São Paulo, Brazil. Deeply rooted in the rich traditions of Afro-Brazilian music, she has become known for her expressive vocal style that artfully blends power and softness, drawing comparisons to iconic samba singers such as Elizeth Cardoso and Clara Nunes. Her early exposure to the vibrant samba scene of São Paulo shaped her musical sensibilities and inspired a lifelong dedication to the genre.

Moreira's career gained momentum through her performances at major venues in São Paulo, most notably during her seven-year residency leading the celebrated Monday night sessions at Ó do Borogodó, a renowned bar and concert hall. Her original show, 'Santa Segunda,' showcased a repertoire developed during this period, highlighting her ability to honor samba's roots while infusing her own creative voice. Her discography includes the albums 'Direito de Sambar' (2006) and 'Cordão' (2012), both of which reflect her commitment to preserving and evolving the samba tradition.

Beyond her solo work, Adriana Moreira has participated in projects that celebrate the diversity of Brazilian black music, including performances at the Malungo Festival, where she contributed to a showcase of Afro-rooted genres such as jongo, capoeira, and partido-alto. Her artistry and dedication have established her as a respected figure in contemporary samba, admired for her authentic interpretations and her role in sustaining Brazil's cultural heritage.
