## Biography
The Iguanas are a roots rock band formed in 1989 in New Orleans, Louisiana. Known for their eclectic sound that blends Cajun, zydeco, Chicano rock, R&B, and various Latin styles, the band has become a fixture of the New Orleans music scene. Their music is characterized by a deep groove and infectious rhythms, drawing from America's diverse musical heritage. Over the years, The Iguanas have released several albums, performed at major festivals, and contributed to film soundtracks, earning a reputation for energetic live shows and genre-bending creativity. Their career has spanned over three decades, with a consistent presence in both regional and national music circles.

## Fun Facts
- The Iguanas have performed at the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival and released a live album from their 2019 appearance.
- Their music has been featured in the documentary 'New Orleans Music in Exile,' discussing the impact of Hurricane Katrina on the band.
- The band's sound incorporates elements from Cajun, zydeco, Chicano rock, and Latin styles, making them unique in the roots rock genre.
- The Iguanas have been together for nearly 30 years, maintaining a consistent lineup of Rod Hodges, Joe Cabral, Rene Coman, and Doug Garrison.
