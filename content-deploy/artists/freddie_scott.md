## Biography
Freddie Scott was born on April 24, 1933, in Providence, Rhode Island. His early exposure to music came through his grandmother, Sally Jones, a gospel singer with the Gospel Keyes. Scott initially pursued a career in medicine but eventually turned to music. He began writing and recording songs in the late 1950s, working with notable figures like Zelma 'Zell' Sanders at J&S Records. His breakthrough came with the song 'Hey Girl,' which reached #10 on the Billboard charts in 1963. This success led to collaborations with prominent songwriters such as Gerry Goffin and Carole King. Scott's most notable work was with Bert Berns at Shout Records, where he recorded the hit 'Are You Lonely for Me' in 1966, which topped the R&B charts for four weeks. Despite his significant contributions to soul music, Scott's career was somewhat overshadowed by the death of Bert Berns, a key collaborator. Freddie Scott passed away on June 4, 2007.

Freddie Scott's musical style spanned various genres, including doo-wop, uptown soul, and contemporary soul. His recordings often featured arrangements by top industry professionals like Gary Sherman. Scott was known for his powerful and soulful tenor voice, which was showcased in his work with Shout Records. His legacy is marked by his ability to adapt to changing musical trends while maintaining a distinctive soulful sound.

Scott's impact on the music industry is evident in his enduring legacy as a soul legend. His work with Bert Berns and other notable figures has been celebrated by soul aficionados. Despite not achieving widespread fame beyond his peak in the 1960s, Freddie Scott remains a significant figure in the history of soul music.
