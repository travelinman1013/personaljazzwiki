## Biography
The Dead Rocks is a surf music trio from São Carlos, Brazil, formed in May 2002 by Johnny Crash on electric guitar, Marky Wildstone on drums, and Paul Punk on bass. The band quickly gained popularity in the Brazilian surf rock scene, blending classic surf instrumentals with rockabilly influences, and has released at least six albums, including Surf Explosão and International Brazilian Surfs.[1][2][6] Known for their energetic live performances, they have toured extensively across Brazil and internationally in countries such as Germany, Argentina, Belgium, Spain, France, Hungary, Italy, England, Portugal, and the Czech Republic, performing at festivals, parties, pubs, and major events.[3]

Over more than two decades, The Dead Rocks have built a strong reputation in the global surf music community, earning praise from critics like Phil Dirt of Reverb Central and Hunter of Storm Surge of Reverb for their potent, sixties-inspired sound. Their discography includes collaborations and splits with international artists, and they have appeared in films, television, and numerous publications worldwide. Despite a modest Spotify popularity score of 5, their dedication to the genre has resulted in releases on labels from various countries, solidifying their status as one of Brazil's leading surf trios.[2][3][5]

The band's legacy endures through their consistent output and international reach, contributing to the revival and preservation of surf rock outside its traditional strongholds. They continue to captivate audiences with unforgettable shows and high-quality recordings that capture the essence of surf culture.[3]
