## Biography
The Vitamin String Quartet (VSQ) is a Los Angeles-based collective of musicians known for their classical crossover renditions of modern rock, pop, and other genres. Founded in 1999 by Leo Flynn of Vitamin Records, VSQ has evolved into a versatile ensemble that features a rotating cast of skilled violinists, violists, cellists, and sometimes additional instrumentalists. Their career began with string tributes to iconic bands like Metallica and Radiohead, expanding to cover a wide range of artists across genres. A significant breakthrough came with their involvement in Netflix's 'Bridgerton,' where their string versions of contemporary pop hits became fan favorites. Over the years, VSQ has produced over 400 albums, transforming hits from artists like Lady Gaga, Adele, and Kanye West into classical masterpieces. Their music has been featured in numerous TV shows and films, solidifying their place in contemporary music. VSQ's impact extends beyond their music, as they have helped popularize classical crossover and inspired a new generation of musicians to explore this genre.
