## Biography
Wayne Maureau is a versatile drummer and composer born and raised in New Orleans, Louisiana, a city renowned for its rich musical heritage. Exposed to the vibrant sounds of New Orleans from an early age, Maureau began playing drums at just five years old, quickly immersing himself in the diverse rhythms and styles that define the Crescent City[8]. Over the course of more than three decades, he has become a fixture in the local music scene, celebrated for his ability to seamlessly navigate genres ranging from jazz and funk to blues, gospel, and Latin jazz[1][4][6].

Maureau's career has been marked by collaborations with many of New Orleans' top artists, both as a live performer and a recording musician. He is particularly known for his work with the Soul Project NOLA, where his dynamic drumming forms the backbone of the band's infectious grooves and precision arrangements[6]. His versatility and deep understanding of rhythm have made him a sought-after musician, called upon for a wide range of projects and performances that showcase the city's musical diversity[4][5].

Beyond his contributions as a performer, Maureau has also made his mark as a composer, most notably with his album 'At The Water's Edge,' which reflects his appreciation for the ebb and flow of life and music in New Orleans[9]. His legacy is that of a dedicated artist who embodies the spirit of his hometown, continually evolving while honoring the traditions that shaped him.
