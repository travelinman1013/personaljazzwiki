## Biography
Crow Black Chicken is a blues-rock power trio originating from Cork and Clonmel, Ireland, formed in 2009 by vocalist/guitarist Christy O'Hanlon, bassist Stephen McGrath, and drummer Gev Barrett. The band initially started as a covers act, drawing on classic blues and rock influences, but quickly transitioned to writing original material that showcased their gritty, southern American-inspired sound. Their early years saw them performing on the BBC Introducing stage at Glastonbury in 2011, a significant milestone that helped establish their reputation on the live circuit.

Their debut album, 'Electric Soup,' was released in 2012 to critical acclaim, followed by 'Rumble Shake' in 2014, which debuted at number 12 on the Irish Albums Chart. Subsequent releases, including 'Pariah Brothers' (2016) and 'Ghost Dance' (2025), have cemented their place in the Irish and European blues-rock scenes. The band is celebrated for their relentless touring schedule, energetic live performances, and a dedicated fanbase known as the 'Crow Black Chicken Army.'
