## Biography
Derrick 'Smoker' Freeman is a New Orleans-based virtuoso percussionist, vocalist, pianist, songwriter, DJ, and music industry veteran known for his dynamic presence in the local scene. He leads the soul-funk ensemble Soul Brass Band and the hip-hop collective Smokers World, blending New Orleans jazz traditions with pop, funk, and hip-hop elements. His solo work with Smokers World began in 2007 as a collective dedicated to pop and funk rooted in the New Orleans jazz tradition, expanding his career in 2012 with further projects.[1][2][3]

Freeman's career development showcases his versatility across genres and roles, from drumming and songwriting to DJing. He has been a key member of Kermit Ruffins and the BBQ Swingers, contributing his animated and fun-loving style to their performances, as well as participating in groups like Low End Theory Players. His leadership in Soul Brass Band highlights his soul-funk innovations, while Smokers World represents his hip-hop explorations, establishing him as a multifaceted figure in New Orleans music.[3][4][5][6]

Freeman's musical style fuses traditional New Orleans brass band sounds with modern funk, soul, hip-hop, and pop, creating energetic, genre-blending performances that reflect the city's vibrant cultural heritage. His legacy lies in bridging jazz traditions with contemporary urban sounds, influencing the local scene through his collectives and collaborations.
