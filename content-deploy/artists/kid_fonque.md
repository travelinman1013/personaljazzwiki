## Biography
Allan Nicoll, known professionally as Kid Fonque, is a Scottish-born, South African-adopted DJ, producer, label head, and broadcaster who has played a pivotal role in shaping South African electronic music and club culture for over two decades. Born in Scotland, Nicoll moved to South Africa at the age of four, where he developed a deep passion for music and DJing. His career began in the vibrant Johannesburg scene, notably at the legendary 206 Live, and he quickly established himself as a leading figure in deep house and electronic music. Over the years, Kid Fonque has been instrumental in promoting underground and alternative sounds, founding the influential label Stay True Sounds in 2016, which has become a platform for showcasing the best of South Africa’s deep house and afro house talent. His work as a presenter on national radio and television further cemented his status as a tastemaker and advocate for quality house music.

Kid Fonque’s musical style is rooted in deep house, afro house, and afro tech, blending local rhythms with global influences. His approach is characterized by a commitment to authenticity and quality, often prioritizing artistic integrity over commercial success. In recent years, he has expanded his reach internationally, taking on the role of Head of A&R for the renowned UK-based Defected Records, where he continues to champion South African talent on a global stage. Through his label, radio shows, and international collaborations, Kid Fonque has left an indelible mark on the electronic music landscape, both in South Africa and beyond.

## Fun Facts
- Kid Fonque began his DJ career by carrying a bag of 20 CDs to college and playing music for people, a testament to his early passion and dedication.
- He is known for his DIY philosophy, inspired by British independent labels like Ninja Tune and Mo Wax, which he brought with him from Scotland.
- Kid Fonque’s label, Stay True Sounds, releases an average of three EPs per month, reflecting his commitment to supporting a steady stream of new talent.
- He has presented his award-winning Selective Styles show exclusively for Defected Broadcasting House, bringing South African underground sounds to a global audience.
