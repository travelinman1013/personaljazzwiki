## Biography
Robin Barnes, known as the **Songbird of New Orleans**, was born and raised in the Lower 9th Ward of New Orleans, Louisiana, shuttling between her parents' and grandparents' houses where she sang the songs they enjoyed.[3][4][7] A performer since age six, she grew up singing with her family's band and in local settings, developing her craft early in life amid the city's rich musical traditions.[5][7] Her voice, powerful in both whispers and bellows, surprised and delighted audiences from the start.[5]

Barnes has evolved into a contemporary crossover singer blending jazz, modern funky jazz, R&B, and New Orleans rhythms, earning acclaim as an award-winning artist and city ambassador.[1][2][6] She performs solo, with her band **Robin Barnes & the Fiya Birds**—evoking Chaka Khan meets Ella Fitzgerald—and as the soulful R&B duo **Da Lovebirds** alongside her husband Pat Casey, delivering sultry, intimate vibes with electric live energy.[1][6] Having performed worldwide while remaining rooted in New Orleans, her style proudly incorporates local traditions.[2][4]

As a lasting figure in New Orleans music, Barnes carries the official distinction of **Songbird of New Orleans** and continues to champion the city's sounds through her dynamic performances and projects.[1][2][6]
