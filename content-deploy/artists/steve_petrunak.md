## Biography
Steve Petrunak is an accomplished guitarist, composer, instructor, clinician, and author with nearly 50 years of experience in Catholic Church music ministry. His career began in the Detroit area, where he became known for his leadership in church music and his dedication to music education. Over the decades, Petrunak has recorded and produced more than 1,000 guitar-based instrumental tracks spanning a wide variety of genres, and his works are available across major streaming platforms. He has also authored several instructional books for GIA Publications and has taught over 150 guitar students throughout his career.

Petrunak's influence extends beyond performance and teaching; he has served as Artistic Director and Primary Conductor for choirs exceeding 200 voices, including leading performances at Carnegie Hall's Perelman Stage in Stern Auditorium. Nationally recognized for his leadership, he was President and CEO of the National Association of Pastoral Musicians from 2017 to 2020, and has held board positions with both the NPM and the American Guild of Music. Locally, he is a faculty member at Sacred Heart Major Seminary in Detroit and serves as Director of Music at St. Blase Catholic Church in Sterling Heights, Michigan. His commitment to liturgical music is further reflected in his role as a founding member of the Catholic Liturgical Ensemble Formation, a non-profit supporting ensemble church musicians.
