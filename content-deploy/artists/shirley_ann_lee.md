## Biography
Shirley Ann Lee is a gospel singer and pianist born at the tail end of the Great Depression in the United States, emerging from obscurity as a notable figure in 1960s gospel music. She developed her talents as a singer and pianist during a challenging era, contributing to the Michigan gospel scene alongside groups like Voices of Deliverance and Gospel Supremes. Her work was later rescued from obscurity and showcased on CD compilations, highlighting her role in preserving and performing gospel traditions.[4][6]
