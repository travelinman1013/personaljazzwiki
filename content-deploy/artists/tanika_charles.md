## Biography
Tanika Charles is a Canadian soul and R&B singer born and raised in Edmonton, Alberta, to African-Caribbean parents.  Her musical journey began in Edmonton, though specific details about her early musical training or influences are not provided in the source.  She relocated to Toronto in the late 2000s after securing a position as a backing vocalist for Bedouin Soundclash, a pivotal moment that launched her professional career.  Prior to her debut album, she performed solo under the stage name Ms. Chawlz, releasing the EP *What! What? What!?* in 2010.

Charles' career progressed significantly with her debut full-length album, *Soul Run*, released in 2016.  Preceding the album, she released the singles "Soul Run" and "Two Steps," both of which received airplay on CBC Radio 2 and charted on the Radio 2 Top 20.  The album's release was supported by a tour and an appearance on CBC Radio One's *Q*.  *Soul Run* garnered significant recognition, earning a longlist nomination for the 2016 Polaris Music Prize and a Juno Award shortlist nomination for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year in 2017.  Beyond her solo work, Charles has provided backing vocals for notable artists including Johnny Reid, Serena Ryder, and Emmanuel Jal, and also appeared on television shows such as CBC Kids, *Bomb Girls*, *Less Than Kind*, *Workin' Moms*, *Rookie Blue*, *Seed*, and *Saving Hope*. In 2019, she released her second album, *The Gumption*, on Record Kicks.

Charles' musical style is firmly rooted in soul and R&B, though specific details about her innovations or unique stylistic elements are not provided in the source.  The success of her albums and her appearances on various television programs indicate a growing presence in the Canadian music scene.
