## Biography
Derrick 'Kabuki' Shezbie is a celebrated trumpeter from New Orleans, Louisiana, whose musical journey began in childhood. Initially starting out on drums, Shezbie switched to trumpet before his teenage years, quickly developing a reputation for his vibrant sound and technical prowess[3]. By the age of 19, he had already made a significant mark on the jazz scene, releasing his debut album 'Spodie's Back' in May 1994 under Quincy Jones' Qwest Records, which showcased his deep roots in New Orleans jazz and his ability to blend tradition with innovation[2][6].

Shezbie is best known for his tenure with the legendary Rebirth Brass Band, where his energetic trumpet playing helped define the group's sound and contributed to the revival and global spread of New Orleans brass band music[4]. Over the years, Shezbie has become recognized for his commitment to preserving the city's musical heritage, notably through projects such as 'Derrick Shezbie and the Ghost of Buddy Bolden.' This tribute album, released in 2020, was a collaborative effort to recreate the sound and spirit of Buddy Bolden, a foundational figure in early jazz, and involved extensive research and authentic New Orleans musicianship[1][8].

Shezbie's musical style is deeply rooted in the traditions of New Orleans jazz, characterized by expressive improvisation, rhythmic drive, and a soulful approach that bridges gospel, blues, and early jazz. His legacy is marked by both his performance career and his dedication to mentoring younger musicians, ensuring the continuity of New Orleans' vibrant musical culture.

## Fun Facts
- Shezbie was still a teenager when he released his debut album 'Spodie's Back' in 1994[2][6].
- He began his musical journey on drums before switching to trumpet prior to his teens[3].
- For the 'Ghost of Buddy Bolden' album, Shezbie and his collaborators assembled the band in one day and recorded the album after only two days of rehearsal to mimic the spontaneous style of early jazz bands[1].
- Shezbie is nicknamed 'Kabuki,' a moniker that reflects his energetic stage presence[4].
