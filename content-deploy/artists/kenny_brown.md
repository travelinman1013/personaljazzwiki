## Biography
Kennyon Brown is a New Zealand-born singer, songwriter, and producer of Samoan descent whose genre-blending sound fuses R&B, reggae, and island influences. Born in Auckland, Brown spent his formative years in Long Beach, California, before relocating to Sydney, Australia, where he is currently based. Growing up in a multicultural environment, he was exposed to a wide range of musical styles, which shaped his unique approach to songwriting and performance.

Brown began his music career in the early 2010s, initially gaining recognition through online platforms like SoundCloud during the rise of digital streaming. His early work was characterized by smooth vocals, heartfelt lyrics, and a commitment to representing his Polynesian roots. Over the years, he has become known for pushing boundaries within the R&B and reggae genres, collaborating with other Pacific artists and building a loyal following across Australasia and the Pacific diaspora. Brown's music often explores themes of love, identity, and resilience, resonating deeply with fans from similar backgrounds.

As his career developed, Kennyon Brown established himself as a key figure in the NZ reggae and Pacific R&B scenes, performing at major festivals and touring internationally. His cross-cultural experiences and dedication to his craft have made him an influential voice for young Polynesian artists seeking to bridge traditional and contemporary sounds.
