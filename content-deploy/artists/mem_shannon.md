## Biography
Mem Shannon is an acclaimed American blues guitarist, singer, and songwriter, born in 1959 in New Orleans, Louisiana. Growing up in the heart of one of America’s most musically rich cities, Shannon was deeply influenced by the vibrant sounds of New Orleans, which would later become a hallmark of his eclectic style[6]. Before launching his professional music career, Shannon spent 15 years working as a cab driver on Bourbon Street, an experience that profoundly shaped his storytelling and songwriting approach[3][5].

Shannon’s breakthrough came in 1995 with the release of his debut album, 'A Cab Driver’s Blues,' which drew directly from his experiences behind the wheel and featured actual recorded conversations with his passengers[2][3]. His music blends traditional blues with New Orleans funk, jazz, rock, and soul, resulting in a distinctive sound that pushes the boundaries of the genre[1][4]. Over the years, Shannon has continued to record and perform with his band, The Membership, earning a reputation for his insightful lyrics, inventive guitar work, and authentic representation of New Orleans blues. His legacy lies in his ability to merge personal narrative with broader social commentary, making him a respected figure in contemporary blues[4][5].
