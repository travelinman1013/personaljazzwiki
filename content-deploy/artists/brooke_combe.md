## Biography
Brooke Combe is a Scottish singer-songwriter who discovered her love for music at the age of five, starting with a toy drum kit. She later expanded her musical repertoire to include saxophone, trombone, guitar, bass, and piano. Influenced by '90s R&B and Motown, her unique voice was shaped by artists like Amy Winehouse and Fleetwood Mac. Combe's breakthrough year was 2021, where she won the Breakthrough Female prize at the Scottish Music Awards. Her debut single 'Are You With Me?' topped the Official Vinyl Singles chart in 2022. She has supported notable bands like The Courteeners and The Snuts, and has performed at several UK festivals. In 2023, she released her debut album 'Black is The New Gold', which explores her heritage and personal journey. However, recent information indicates her actual debut album is 'Dancing At The Edge Of The World', released in January 2025, collaborating with James Skelly of The Coral. This album showcases her soulful voice and orchestral arrangements, drawing comparisons to Michael Kiwanuka and Leon Bridges.

Combe's musical style is characterized by soulful vocals and a blend of genres, often drawing comparisons to legendary artists. Her music often reflects themes of personal growth and heritage, making her a standout in the contemporary music scene. With her rising popularity, she continues to tour and perform at major festivals, solidifying her position as a promising young artist.

Throughout her career, Combe has been encouraged by her family and teachers, particularly her music teacher Grace Jack, who recognized her vocal talent early on. This support has been instrumental in her musical development and success.
