## Biography
Roosevelt Melvin "Booba" Barnes was born on September 25, 1936, in Longwood, Mississippi, and became a prominent figure in Delta blues music. He began his musical journey at an exceptionally young age, starting to play harmonica on a plantation at just seven years old. By age seventeen, Barnes had decided to pursue music professionally and became a regular performer in the clubs along Greenville's famous Nelson Street, establishing himself as a skilled harmonica player and guitarist in the Mississippi blues scene.

Throughout his career, Barnes developed into a respected Delta blues guitarist and vocalist, eventually leading his own band and operating his own club. He became known for his authentic Delta blues style, drawing from the rich musical traditions of the Mississippi region. Barnes maintained an active presence in the blues community, performing regularly and contributing to the preservation of Delta blues traditions during a period when the genre faced declining popularity.

Booba Barnes passed away on April 2, 1996, in Chicago, Illinois, at the age of 59. Though he remained relatively obscure in mainstream music circles, he is remembered as a dedicated blues musician who kept the Delta blues tradition alive through his performances and leadership of his own musical ensemble.
