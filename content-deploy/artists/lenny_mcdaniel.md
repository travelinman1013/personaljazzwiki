## Biography
Lenny McDaniel was a New Orleans-born singer-songwriter and guitarist whose work bridged the soulful traditions of his hometown with a distinctive blend of rock, blues, and R&B. Born in 1949, McDaniel began his musical journey in his youth, developing his skills as both a vocalist and multi-instrumentalist. His early exposure to the vibrant New Orleans music scene deeply influenced his songwriting and performance style, which often featured piano and organ textures alongside his guitar work.

McDaniel's career spanned several decades, during which he released multiple albums, including 'Bad For Me,' 'Worth the Price,' and 'Tired Angels.' His music was marked by memorable hooks, soulful vocals, and a balance between lighthearted rock and more introspective, brooding themes. McDaniel was celebrated for his ability to infuse his songs with the spirit of New Orleans, as heard in tracks like 'Lord Knows I Try' and the gospel-tinged title track 'Tired Angels.' Despite his talent and critical acclaim, McDaniel remained a cult favorite, with his songs covered by artists such as Marva Wright and Debbie Davies. He passed away in 2015 at the age of 65, leaving behind a legacy as a respected figure in the New Orleans music community.
