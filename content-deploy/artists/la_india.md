## Biography
Linda Bell Viera Caballero, known professionally as La India, was born on March 9, 1969, in Río Piedras, Puerto Rico, and raised in the South Bronx, New York City. Her grandmother gave her the nickname 'La India' due to her brown skin and long black hair, a moniker that would later become synonymous with her musical legacy. La India began her career at age 16, joining the Latin freestyle group TKA before transitioning to salsa music, where she found her true calling. Her breakthrough came in 1992 with the album 'Llego La India via Eddie Palmieri,' produced by salsa legend Eddie Palmieri, which established her as a leading voice in the genre. Over the years, La India has released numerous hit albums, including 'Dicen Que Soy,' 'Sobre el Fuego,' and 'Soy Diferente,' showcasing her versatility and emotional depth. Her music blends salsa with elements of R&B, Latin pop, and house, resonating with audiences worldwide and especially with women for its empowering lyrics and heartfelt performances.

La India's career has been marked by collaborations with some of the biggest names in Latin music, including Celia Cruz, Marc Anthony, and Tito Puente. Her powerful voice and emotive style have earned her the title 'La Princesa de la Salsa' (The Princess of Salsa). She has won multiple awards, including a Latin Grammy for Best Salsa Album, and has been recognized for her contributions to Latin music history. La India's influence extends beyond her recordings; her performances and advocacy have made her a role model for aspiring Latin artists.
