## Biography
Amy Gallatin was born in Muscle Shoals, Alabama, but was raised in the western United States, where she developed a deep connection to country, folk, and bluegrass music. Early in her career, she worked for approximately ten years as a riding instructor, backcountry cook, and campfire musician at guest ranches across Washington, Montana, and Idaho. These formative years in the American West significantly shaped her musical identity and performance style. Since 2000, Gallatin has performed with her musical partner Roger as part of the acoustic ensemble Stillwaters, blending country music and bluegrass into a distinctive acoustic style that has earned them recognition as one of the top acts in their genre. Her bell-like voice and versatility across multiple musical styles—including country, folk, bluegrass, and western swing—have made her a respected figure in the folk and bluegrass music communities. Gallatin has been recognized as a key figure in the growing popularity of bluegrass and folk music, and she remains devoted to the type of music she most enjoys performing.
