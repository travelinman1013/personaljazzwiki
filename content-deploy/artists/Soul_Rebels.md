## Biography
The Soul Rebels, also known as the Soul Rebels Brass Band, are a renowned New Orleans brass band that emerged in 1991. Founded by percussionists Lumar LeBlanc and Derrick Moss, the group began as a departure from traditional brass bands, aiming to incorporate contemporary pop music into their sound. They drew inspiration from funk, rap, and hip-hop, blending these styles with the rich brass tradition of New Orleans. The band's early years were marked by their unique blend of genres, which quickly gained them popularity among music lovers and fellow musicians alike. Over the years, The Soul Rebels have collaborated with a wide range of artists, from Nas and GZA to Metallica and Living Colour's Corey Glover, showcasing their adaptability and innovative approach to music. Their albums, such as 'Unlock Your Mind' (2012) and 'Poetry in Motion' (2019), have been critically acclaimed and fan-pleasing hits. The band continues to tour extensively and maintain a strong presence in the music scene, both locally and globally. Their eclectic live shows are known for their energetic and party-like atmosphere, blending horns and drums in a deep funk style. The Soul Rebels have also been involved in scoring music for films, such as a Walt Disney movie in 2023, further expanding their musical reach. The band's members include Lumar LeBlanc, Derrick Moss, Julian Gosin, Marcus Hubbard, Corey Peyton, Paul Robertson, Erion Williams, and Manuel Perkins Jr., each contributing to the band's dynamic sound. The Soul Rebels' legacy is built on their ability to merge traditional New Orleans brass with modern genres, creating a unique sound that resonates with audiences worldwide. This blend of styles has not only preserved the city's musical heritage but also evolved it, making them a pivotal part of New Orleans' modern brass wave.
