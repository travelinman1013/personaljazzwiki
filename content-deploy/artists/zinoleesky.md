## Biography
Zinoleesky, born Oniyide Azeez on April 21, 2000, in Agege, Lagos State, Nigeria, is a prominent Nigerian singer, songwriter, and rapper celebrated for his contributions to the afrobeats and street pop genres[1][3]. Raised in a vibrant yet challenging neighborhood, he attended Lively Kiddies Nursery and Primary School and later Boys Secondary School in Agege, where his passion for music began to flourish[1][3]. His early career was marked by viral freestyle videos shared on social media with childhood friends Lil Frosh and Ibradosky, which caught the attention of established artists and propelled him into the spotlight[1][3][4].

Zinoleesky's breakthrough came in 2017 when his online presence attracted significant attention, including a pivotal moment when Kizz Daniel followed him on Instagram and reposted his work[3][4]. In 2019, he declined a record deal from Davido's DMW label, choosing instead to sign with Naira Marley's Marlian Records[1][3]. His debut EP, 'Chrome Eccentric,' released in 2020, featured hit tracks like 'Ma Pariwo' and 'Kilofeshe,' which charted on Apple Music and YouTube[1]. He continued to build his discography with collaborations and remixes, notably with Mayorkun and Busiswa, and released his first full EP, 'Grit and Lust,' in December 2022[1].

Musically, Zinoleesky is known for blending afrobeats, street pop, and afropiano, infusing his work with relatable narratives and energetic rhythms. His legacy is defined by his rapid ascent from social media freestyles to mainstream success, inspiring a new generation of Nigerian artists with his authenticity and resilience[1][3][4].
